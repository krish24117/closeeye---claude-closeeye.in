Ask was built for the 400th day.
Connect was built for the first 60 seconds.

Asking which one is “better” hides the fact that they are answering different questions. One of them is better for the user in a hurry. The other is better for the product being understood. Only one of those two problems gets harder if we ignore it.

A · Ask (closeeye.in, in-app)

Wins the task. Loses the category.

Nothing is ever hidden: six chips, a composer, and an urgent door on screen at all times. It is also, unmistakably, a chatbot — which is the one thing we have said Close Eye is not.

B · Connect (/connect)

Wins the category. Loses the blank page.

Ruled paper and a serif prompt say “this is not ChatGPT” before a word is read. Then it hands you an empty sheet and no visible way to shout for help.

The deciding insightThe bug in Ask isn’t the layout

Look at what Ask actually said. The user typed “How is siyah doing” and got back “I’m not sure who Siyah is or what’s going on with them — could you tell me a bit more?” — clipped mid-sentence at “and what h…”.

That is the product whose entire thesis is that it knows your family, announcing that it does not know your family. And the chat frame is what makes it land as a let-down: a bubble is a turn in a conversation, and a conversation that begins “I don’t know who that is” has already gone badly.

The ledger turns “I don’t know yet” into trust. The chat bubble turns the same words into disappointment. Identical fact. Opposite feeling.

That is the real argument for the desk, and it is not an aesthetic one. An open circle beside Her health — I don’t know yet is a system showing its work. The same sentence in a speech bubble is a system making an excuse. We are going to be uncertain constantly — it is a deterministic engine that refuses to invent. So we should choose the frame in which uncertainty is a feature.

The hybridA writing desk that remembers

Don’t merge the layouts — you’d get a chat with a paper texture. Merge the principles: keep Connect’s frame, and take the three things Ask is genuinely better at.

The chips are the ledger’s open circles. Not a menu of prompts — the questions the engine genuinely hasn’t answered, made tappable. Recognition over recall, zero chatbot smell, nothing invented.

The three moves

1 · The urgent door is always present — and is never a chip among chips. Ask gets this right and Connect doesn’t. Safety must never require you to compose a sentence first. But it belongs at the desk’s edge, quiet and permanent, not floating in a list of pleasantries next to “Book another Presence”. A person in a crisis is not choosing from a menu.

2 · The blank page is solved by a borrowed sentence, not by six chips. The starter must be a sentence a person would write — “Amma lives alone in Hyderabad and I worry about her” — tappable to adopt and edit. Not “What made Siyah smile today?”, which is a prompt aimed at a bot and teaches the wrong category. We already ship this as the demo; the hybrid just lets you take the pen from it.

3 · The suggestions ARE the unknowns. This is the move that makes the hybrid worth building. Ask’s chips are invented conversation-starters. The hybrid’s are the ledger’s open circles rendered as buttons — so every tap closes a real gap in what Close Eye knows, the answer is guaranteed to be useful, and the list shrinks as the family graph fills. A menu that gets shorter the better it knows you is a very different promise from a chatbot’s infinite one.

ConsequenceKeep / kill

Keep from Ask

  • The always-visible urgent door — the single best thing on that screen.
  • Recognition over recall. People don’t know what to type. Never punish that.
  • A persistent place (nav) once you’re a member — Connect’s nowhere-ness is right for a stranger, wrong for day 400.

Kill from Ask

  • Speech bubbles. They make honest uncertainty read as failure.
  • The ✨ avatar. It is the universal “I am a chatbot” signifier. Our own mark should speak.
  • “Book another Presence” at chip weight — commerce disguised as conversation, beside “What made Siyah smile today?”
  • The transcript. Replace scrollback with the ledger: the question is “what does Close Eye know about Amma?”, not “what did we say to each other?”
  • The clipped bubble. That one’s just a bug.
