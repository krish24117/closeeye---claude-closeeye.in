# Collaboration & Delegation — Real-Family UAT Protocol (v2)

**Purpose.** Prove that real families can **understand, trust, and complete meaningful responsibilities
with Close Eye without coaching.** This is a *behavioral* gate, not a technical one. We do **not** care
whether anyone can explain "Trusted Network" or any other architecture — we care whether they
**naturally involve the right person and finish the responsibility.** If they succeed without ever using
our words, that is a pass.

**Facilitator rule.** Do **not** explain the UI or point to anything. Silence is data. If a participant
is stuck ~30s, note it, offer the *smallest* possible hint, and record that a hint was needed. A task
that needs a hint is not a clean pass.

---

## Recruit (6–8 families)

Spread over the segments that struggled in simulation — not volume:

- **≥3 low-technical-ability** (the caregiver's parent, a non-app-native adult).
- **≥2 aged 60+.**
- **≥2 first-time** (never seen Close Eye) and **≥2 returning.**
- At least one **non-health** scenario (property / finance / legal) alongside elder-care.

~30 min per session, screen-recorded with consent, think-aloud.

---

## Scenario A — Cold start (the information-architecture test)

**Run this first, before any guided task.** Start the participant on **Home**. Say only:

> "Your mother isn't feeling well. Show me what you would do."

Do **not** mention Connect. Do **not** say where to tap. Observe.

- **Pass:** they naturally find Connect, ask, and reach a grounded answer + a next step — unaided.
- **Signal:** if most participants discover Connect on their own, the IA is working. If they wander
  through the app, **Home needs refinement** (report it — do not fix mid-UAT).

---

## Scenario B — Completing a responsibility

Give each as a *goal*, never as UI steps. Mark **Pass** only if completed unaided.

| # | Task (say it like this) | Pass = | Watch for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "You've read the answer — what would you do next?" | Engages a Recommended Next Step **unprompted** | Do they *see* it? Does it feel like the obvious next move? |
| 2 | "Let your sister know about this." | Completes Share (adds her inline if needed) | Any hesitation picking/adding a person? |
| 3 | "Get a doctor to look at it." | Completes Invite with a purpose | Does the purpose line feel natural? Does "will receive" reassure? |
| 4 | "Ask someone to pick up her medicines by tomorrow." | Completes Assign | Is Today/Tomorrow/This-week obvious? |
| 5 | "Involve the right person to help with **[their real scenario]**." | Finds & picks the appropriate person **without the concept explained** | Do they reach for the right person confidently? |
| 6 | "Come back tomorrow — where would you go to check on this?" | Returns to the in-progress responsibility unaided | Do they know where it lives? |

> Note task 5: success is **confidently involving the right person**, not naming or explaining any
> screen. If they get the right person to the right job, it's a pass — words don't matter.

---

## After **every** task — ask these five

Capture answers verbatim; they reveal what completion rates miss.

1. **"Without looking back — what just happened?"**
2. **"Who is responsible now?"**
3. **"If you wanted to continue tomorrow, where would you go?"**
4. **"Did anything surprise you?"**
5. **"If this were your own family, would you actually use this? Why?"**

---

## Record per participant

- **Completion** per task: Pass / Pass-with-hint / Fail.
- **Time** to complete each task (goal stated → done).
- **Hesitations** (pauses >5s) and **wrong turns** (taps that didn't advance the goal).
- **Misread words** — quote them (which labels did they misunderstand?).
- **Surprises & expectation gaps** — verbatim, from the five questions.
- **The verdict question** (#5) — would they use it, and the *why*.
- **One-word feel:** did they describe it as "simple" / "straightforward" — or something else?

---

## Go / No-Go — authorize Phase 4 only if

1. **≥90%** complete the **primary care journey** (cold start → answer → at least one meaningful next action).
2. **≥85%** complete the **collaboration flow** (share / invite / assign) **without assistance**.
3. **No participant** gets **permanently stuck** on any task.
4. **Most** participants can **return to an in-progress responsibility** without guidance (task 6 / question 3).
5. **Most** participants describe the experience as **"simple"** or **"straightforward."**

- **All five met →** Proceed to Phase 4 (task details, object threads, notifications).
- **1–2 missed, fixable copy/flow →** refine those, re-test only the failing tasks.
- **Care journey <90%, collaboration <85%, or anyone permanently stuck →** stop and escalate; do not proceed.

---

## Notes for the facilitator

- We are **not** scoring architecture comprehension. A participant who never says "Trusted Network"
  but gets the right person to the right job has **passed** the thing that matters.
- Watch the vulnerable segments hardest — low-tech and 60+. The simulation says the walls are gone;
  only real people confirm whether the forms are effortless enough for them.
- Deferred, do **not** fix mid-UAT: care-framed copy on non-health domains (a minor comfort issue for
  legal/property/finance users). Note if anyone trips on it; batch the fix afterward.
